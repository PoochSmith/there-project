Designing National Park City

What if cities were designed as spaces for both people and wildlife to thrive? What if we thought like coots or peregrines? This London Design Festival, There Project takes you on an audio journey through one of London's iconic landscapes - the 200-year-old Regents Canal. Taking a bird's eye view, we interrogate whether design can help us become more ecocentric. Starting at the Islington Tunnel, listeners be guided by 15-year-old conservationist Kabir Kaul, along the way hearing from designers, thinkers, activists, and residents who are transforming our place in nature, and natureâ€™s place in our National Park City.

Catch this audio journey on your walk between LDF destinations or tune in from anywhere else in the world for an opportunity to take a deep breath, transport yourself to the canal, and think about your own relationship with wildlife in the city. We encourage everyone who can to go out and enjoy this audio journey on the Canal - recommended starting point is at Islington Tunnel (Colebrook Row, N1 8AP - google maps link) - walking East towards Kingsland Basin Nature Reserve (a leisurely 40mn walk). A smartphone and headphones are needed to access the content.

Created for National Park City for London Design Festival 2020.
